Term Explanation

Overall

Remaining HP % (Rem. HP %)

A ratio of the remaining HP quantity out of the max HP. A percentage, which is the remaining HP devided by the max HP.

To draw an exact line, here we call the HP, which is expressed by a number as usual, "HP quantity" or "HP qty," and call the HP, which is expressed by a ratio, "HP percentage" or "HP %."

Though the max HP qty is contained in the formula for finding the catch probability, it doesn't depend on its size actually. Therefore, the catch probability can be calculated if we get this "remaining HP %." This is true through the entire series, and we've proved each not to need those. See the term explanation of each version.

However, we don't use the formula combined on demonstration in this program, to fit the internal process of the game.

Catch rate

The degree of easiness to catch. The higher the value, the easier to catch. It's defined in each species.

Cumulative catch probability (Cumulative catch prob.)

Against the catch probability, which means the probability of catch on throwing a ball once, it means the probability of catch from the result on thrown a ball many times. You can get it from:

pc = 1 - pb

which pc is the cumulative catch probability, p is the catch probability, and b is the number of balls used.

This is an approach that the more the throws the better the probability of catch.

Gen I: Red, Green, Blue, Yellow

Actual remaining HP %

A remaining HP % actually substituted into the formula of the catch probability. There's a low limit value varies from ball to ball, and the remaining HP percentage less than it is substituted by it.

It means, you can't get any effects however you reduce the HP more than the low limit value.

The low limit value is given from the max HP qty devided by the low limit constant which is the following:

Poké Ball = 3
Great Ball = 2
Ultra Ball = 3

Exceptionally, the max HP % loses all relations when the wild is a specific Pokmon and has no ailment. In this case, a blank is displayed.

Catch rate bonus (C.R. bonus)

It's simply a ball performance in this time, and is the expression:

ÅÄ bonusindex

Decision threshold (Decision thrsh.)

Used on handing down the final decision of the capture. If the generated random value (between 0 to 255) is less than it, then the capture will succeed.

This value comes to directly affect to the catch probability but there's a case that decision of catch is finished before, so these are not necessarily in proportion.
